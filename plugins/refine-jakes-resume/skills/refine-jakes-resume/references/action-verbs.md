# Action Verbs Cheat Sheet

Used by the refine-jakes-resume skill. Organized by **what you actually did** rather than abstract categories. When picking a verb, look for the one that *precisely matches the action*. Multiple verbs per scenario is intentional — don't reuse the same verb on a resume, so you'll want options.

The brief note after each verb is the **signal** it sends. Pick the verb whose signal matches the work.

## You made a project

- **Built**: signals ownership of the whole thing
- **Developed**: signals iterative, technical work
- **Created**: broad; works for anything new
- **Implemented**: signals turning a spec into code
- **Engineered**: signals deliberate technical design
- **Designed**: signals architecture or planning decisions
- **Assembled**: signals composing from parts
- **Constructed**: signals careful building
- **Programmed**: signals direct coding work

## You shipped a real thing

- **Shipped**: strongest; signals real users got it
- **Launched**: signals public release moment
- **Released**: signals a versioned production push
- **Deployed**: signals you got it running on a server
- **Delivered**: signals you carried it to the finish line
- **Operated**: signals you kept it running in production
- **Maintained**: signals ongoing operational work
- **Upgraded**: signals moving to a newer version
- **Executed**: signals you completed a planned rollout

## You made something faster

- **Optimized**: signals targeted improvement with intent
- **Accelerated**: signals speed gains
- **Reduced**: signals less (latency, time, cost); pair with a number
- **Improved**: softer; only use with a number
- **Streamlined**: signals removed steps and friction
- **Profiled**: signals you measured before optimizing
- **Enhanced**: softer improvement signal

## You cleaned up messy code

- **Refactored**: clear engineering term; signals you know good code
- **Simplified**: signals taste and judgment
- **Reorganized**: signals structural work
- **Migrated**: signals moving off a legacy stack
- **Redesigned**: signals rethinking the structure
- **Standardized**: signals setting conventions
- **Unified**: signals consolidating disparate approaches
- **Structured**: signals adding organization where there was none

## You connected two tools

- **Integrated**: standard term for tying systems together
- **Connected**: simpler version of integrated
- **Synced**: signals two-way or scheduled data flow
- **Automated**: signals you removed manual work
- **Orchestrated**: signals coordinated, multi-step pipelines

## You made a backend

- **Built**: ownership signal
- **Designed**: signals architecture decisions
- **Implemented**: signals turning a design into code
- **Deployed**: signals you got it running in production

## You made a frontend

- **Built**: ownership signal
- **Designed**: signals visual or UX decisions
- **Developed**: technical implementation signal
- **Styled**: signals CSS work (narrow; pair with something bigger)

## You made a database

- **Designed**: signals schema work and modeling
- **Modeled**: signals deeper data modeling decisions
- **Queried**: signals SQL fluency
- **Indexed**: signals performance-aware work

## You used AI

- **Built**: ownership over an AI-powered feature
- **Integrated**: signals plumbing the model into an app
- **Fine-tuned**: signals model adaptation work
- **Embedded**: signals vector embedding / retrieval work
- **Ranked**: signals search or relevance work
- **Generated**: signals using the model to produce content
- **Evaluated**: signals you measured model quality

## You fixed a bug

- **Debugged**: signals investigation, not just patching
- **Diagnosed**: signals you found root cause
- **Resolved**: signals you closed it out
- **Patched**: signals a fix (slightly narrower)
- **Triaged**: signals handling a queue of issues
- **Identified**: signals finding the source
- **Reviewed**: signals code review work
- **Solved**: signals end-to-end resolution

## You led people

- **Led**: clear leadership signal
- **Owned**: signals end-to-end accountability
- **Managed**: signals people and coordination work
- **Coordinated**: softer; signals cross-team work
- **Directed**: signals top-down direction
- **Headed**: signals you were the named leader
- **Oversaw**: signals supervisory role
- **Supervised**: signals direct reports
- **Allocated**: signals distributing resources
- **Budgeted**: signals financial planning
- **Orchestrated**: signals coordinating moving parts
- **Organized**: broad organizational signal
- **Planned**: signals forward thinking
- **Prioritized**: signals decision-making under constraints
- **Reviewed**: signals quality control
- **Scheduled**: signals time-management
- **Influenced**: signals indirect persuasion
- **Recruited**: signals bringing people onto the team

## You started something

- **Founded**: strongest; signals you brought it into existence
- **Launched**: signals public or external start
- **Established**: signals formal beginning
- **Formed**: signals you assembled it
- **Introduced**: signals bringing it into the space
- **Invented**: signals you created something new

## You improved a process

- **Streamlined**: signals removed steps and friction
- **Automated**: signals replaced manual work
- **Simplified**: signals taste and judgment
- **Improved**: pair with a metric
- **Increased**: signals growth (pair with a number)
- **Expanded**: signals broadening scope
- **Strengthened**: signals making it more robust
- **Reorganized**: signals structural change
- **Standardized**: signals adding consistency
- **Maximized** / **Minimized**: signals tuning in a direction
- **Reduced**: signals cutting overhead
- **Optimized**: signals targeted tuning

## You measured something

- **Analyzed**: signals investigation and interpretation
- **Benchmarked**: signals quantitative perf comparison
- **Evaluated**: signals judgment based on criteria
- **Validated**: signals you confirmed correctness
- **Verified**: signals you proved it works
- **Audited**: signals systematic review
- **Tested**: signals testing pass
- **Quantified**: signals turning qualitative into numbers

## You wrote docs

- **Documented**: clear and direct
- **Authored**: more weight than "wrote"
- **Standardized**: signals you set conventions
- **Published**: signals docs went out the door
- **Wrote**: simplest; defensible
- **Drafted**: signals initial version
- **Edited**: signals revision of others' work
- **Rewrote**: signals substantial revision
- **Revised**: signals iterative editing
- **Defined**: signals specifying terms or interfaces
- **Specified**: signals laying out requirements
- **Synthesized**: signals combining sources into one document
- **Summarized**: signals condensing

## You mentored or taught someone

- **Taught**: clearest signal for TA work
- **Mentored**: signals one-on-one development of a junior
- **Trained**: signals onboarding people into a process or tool
- **Coached**: signals iterative feedback over time
- **Advised**: signals guidance role
- **Guided**: signals walking someone through
- **Instructed**: signals direct teaching
- **Educated**: signals broad knowledge transfer
- **Demonstrated**: signals showing how
- **Demystified**: signals making complex things accessible
- **Explained**: signals direct teaching
- **Facilitated**: signals enabling others' learning
- **Lectured**: signals formal teaching

## You did research

- **Investigated**: signals deep exploration of an open question
- **Researched**: broad, but always defensible
- **Conducted**: signals running a structured study or experiment
- **Surveyed**: signals breadth across a landscape of approaches
- **Discovered**: signals finding something previously unknown
- **Concluded**: signals end-of-study finding
- **Derived**: signals mathematical or logical deduction
- **Identified**: signals isolating something
- **Interpreted**: signals making sense of data or text
- **Interviewed**: signals talking to people for primary research
- **Modeled**: signals quantitative or formal abstraction
- **Critiqued**: signals analytical review
- **Proved**: signals formal demonstration
- **Predicted**: signals forecasting based on analysis
- **Forecasted**: signals quantitative prediction

## You presented or spoke publicly

- **Presented**: standard; works for demos, talks, lectures
- **Demoed**: signals showing something live (very SWE-flavored)
- **Pitched**: signals persuasion under time pressure
- **Delivered**: signals you carried it through to an audience
- **Persuaded**: signals influence
- **Moderated**: signals running a discussion
- **Lectured**: signals formal teaching presentation

## You won or competed

- **Won**: cleanest; pair with the competition name
- **Placed**: signals top-N finish without the win
- **Competed**: weakest of the three — use only when Won/Placed don't apply
- **Earned**: signals an award or recognition
- **Surpassed**: signals exceeding a benchmark

## You analyzed data

- **Analyzed**: standard; pair with the dataset and finding
- **Visualized**: signals dashboard/chart work
- **Modeled**: signals statistical or ML work
- **Calculated**: signals numerical work
- **Computed**: signals algorithmic computation
- **Quantified**: signals turning observations into numbers
- **Extracted**: signals pulling data from sources
- **Collected**: signals data gathering
- **Processed**: signals data transformation
- **Compiled**: signals assembling into one place
- **Generated**: signals producing data or outputs
- **Predicted**: signals forward-looking inference
- **Interpreted**: signals making sense of results
- **Classified**: signals categorization
- **Profiled**: signals characterizing distribution or behavior
- **Screened**: signals filtering candidates from a larger set

## You collaborated across teams

- **Collaborated**: standard; signals teamwork
- **Coordinated**: signals aligning multiple parties
- **Partnered**: signals working alongside as equals
- **Negotiated**: signals working out terms
- **Mediated**: signals resolving disputes
- **Participated**: signals presence (weakest; use when participation itself is the point — e.g., "participated in design discussions with senior engineers")

## You instrumented or monitored something

- **Monitored**: signals ongoing observation
- **Instrumented**: signals adding measurement to existing code
- **Tracked**: signals following over time
- **Logged**: signals capturing events
- **Recorded**: signals durable capture
